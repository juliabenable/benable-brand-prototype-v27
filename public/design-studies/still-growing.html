Slide 7 · Still growing

Sparkline as visual proof

The slide is currently all words: a claim about growth and a quote that reinforces it. Add a tiny SVG sparkline of views-over-time between the subtitle and the quote — visual proof of the claim, never competing with the text.

Roughly half the value of a creator campaign comes from the months after launch. Evergreen content is the gift that keeps giving.

What changes

  • Add: a small SVG sparkline (full slide width, ~80px tall) between the subtitle and the quote.
  • The curve starts low-left ("launched"), bends sharply upward through a dashed "now" marker, then keeps rising into "3 months" on the right — a visible growth tail.
  • The gradient under the curve (pink → purple, fading down) matches the slide's gradient family.
  • The "now" dot is highlighted so the viewer instantly sees: here's where we are; everything to the right hasn't happened yet.
  • Quote trimmed to one tight sentence — the sparkline is now carrying the "evergreen content is the gift that keeps giving" idea.

Why

The current slide asks the viewer to imagine a growth curve. Adding a real one is the smallest possible change that makes the claim feel substantiated — and it gives the slide a visual anchor it currently lacks.

Keeping it as an SVG (not a chart library) means it's lightweight, scales perfectly, and the shape can be hand-tuned for the exact emotional curve you want (sharp lift at the start, long tail to the right).
