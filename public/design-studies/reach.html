Editorial-stack split

Take the dotted heart out of the centered stack and give it its own column. Let the brand-awareness number be the hero, sized way up. Quote sits under the number, never crowding the heart.

What changes

  • The reach number — 5.5K — becomes the hero. Fraunces 800 italic-ish at ~120px, the same gradient as the current "is ready" accent.
  • The heart moves to its own right column, taking the full right height. It gets to breathe.
  • Quote sits supportingly under the number on the left, with a thin top divider. It can be longer/more substantial without crowding the heart.
  • Headline ("noticed your brand") becomes a short tag under the number, not a competing element.

Why

Right now five elements stack vertically on a fixed 720px slide. Each one competes — and the heart and the quote both want to be the closer. The split lets each element have a clear role: number on the left = "here's the headline stat," heart on the right = "here's what that number feels like."

It also reads like a real editorial page, which matches the direction the rest of the deck (recap, wrap-close, Katie note) is going.
