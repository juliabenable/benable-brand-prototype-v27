Postcard that feels like paper

What changes

  • Tilt: postcard rotated -1.2deg, tape rotated -3deg. Tiny but huge — it stops sitting flat against the slide.
  • Paper grain: a layered repeating-gradient at very low opacity (≈1.2%) plus two corner darken-shadows. Reads as fibrous paper, not flat fill.
  • Inset edge shadow: a soft inner shadow at the edges (inset 0 0 38px 4px rgba(0,0,0,0.045)) gives the postcard a subtle aged-paper depth.
  • Postmark stamp: a small dashed circle top-right with "BENABLE · DEC ·", tilted ~8°, in a faded postal-red. Reads instantly as "this is a real card someone sent."
  • Tape gets a tiny shadow: 2px down, blur 3, 6% alpha — sells that it's stuck on top of the paper, not painted on.

Why

The Kalam handwriting font is doing a lot of work to sell "handwritten note." But the surface it sits on undermines it — flat cream rectangle with a digital tape. The change is all in the paper, not the message.

Tilt + grain + stamp are the three smallest moves that make the biggest "this is paper, not pixels" gain. None require new copy, new font, or layout changes.
